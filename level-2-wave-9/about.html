BE INFORMED

Every visitor, including those not climbing, must check in before entering the facility.

Plan a few extra minutes on your first visit to complete your Participant Agreement and Orientation.

We want everyone entering the facility (climbers, supervisors, adults, spectators) to be fully informed of the risks. We will also provide an orientation to all first-time visitors. From the newest climber to the most experienced professional, we want to make sure you are familiar with everything our facility has to offer!

IMPORTANT: Climbers under 18 must have their parent read and sign the release. If the parent or gaurdian will not be present at check-in, please bring a picture or photocopy of the parent's photo ID for signature verification.

CHOOSE PRODUCT

Individual passes are available for walk-ins, classes for those ready to learn, and memberships for regular climbers. Reservations are required only for groups 7 or more.

Many start climbing with a class or single Day Pass. From there, the more you climb, the more you will save. Our 10-punch pass is a great option for returning visitors. Membership is the best option if you plan to continue climbing once a week or more.

BEST TIME TO CLIMB

Peak times change thoughout the year. Still, there are a few regular program times you may want to avoid for faster check-in. For example, the mezzanine is unavailable during birthday parties on Saturdays from 3:30-5pm and Sundays from 1-3 pm, and after-school programs from 4:30-6:30 Mondays through Fridays during the school year.

CLIMB SMART

As you choose from the available bouldering, top ropes and autobelays, and lead climbing; remember these four simple points of the Climbing Wall Association's ClimbSmart!® program:

  • Climbing is Dangerous
  • Qualified Instruction Is Required
  • Use Equipment According to the Manufacturer's Instructions
  • Your Safety is Your Responsibility

Make sure to ask for a Belay Check before using any equipment for top rope or lead climbing. All visitors must use an approved ABD (Assisted Braking Device) type belay device when belaying another climber. You will be required to demonstrate proper equipment use for a staff member. Need a refresher? Check out our classes.

GROUP RESERVATIONS

Reservations are needed for groups of 7 or more.

Reservations can only be made during off-peak hours and generally require 2-weeks notice. We will help you get the most out of your visit with hired staff to help with check-in, orientations and belaying.

IMPORTANT: The Source is unable to accommodate groups of 7 or more without a reservation. Groups of 7 or more might be turned away during peak hours. Calling ahead or booking online is crucial to avoid being denied entry.

How old do you have to be to to climb?

We recommend climbers be at least 4-6 years of age. Plan for adequate supervision. We recommend at least one adult (over 18) for every 3 climbers under 12. Climbers must be 14 years or older to climb without an adult present.
